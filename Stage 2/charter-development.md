# Stage 2: Project Charter Development 

## 1. Project Objectives

### Purpose

Establish a secure, structured collaboration platform that builds mutual trust between Idea Owners and their Teams. The MVP achieves this by providing automated digital agreements and transparent tracking tools to ensure idea confidentiality while actively protecting the effort and rights of the contributing developers and designers.

### SMART objectives

1. **Automated Digital Agreement (NDA & Role) System**

By the end of the MVP cycle, we will integrate reliable e-signature APIs to automate the signing of NDAs and Charters. To protect both parties' rights, 100% of matched teams must complete this digital agreement before unlocking their private workspaces.

2. **Secure Meeting Scheduler and Archive**

3. **Project Management Dashboard**

By Month 3, we will finalize a project dashboard for UAT (User Acceptance Testing). To clarify roles and mitigate time-management risks, it enables teams to track 5+ milestones, targeting a 90% adoption rate for regular task updates.

## 3. Scopes

### In-Scope

Provide a secure environment to share the ideas and collaborations between users:
- User Profiles: Individual portfolios for all users to showcase skills, roles, and past projects.

- Project Management Dashboard: A centralized hub for teams to create execution plans, track milestones, and manage progress.

- Communication System: Live chat system serving separate purposes.

### Out-of-Scope

- Financial transactions and in-platform payments.

- Legal enforcement (drafting partnership contracts, enforcing NDAs, or pursuing legal action for idea theft).


## 4. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation | Risk Rating |
|------|------|-------------------|
| Lack of commitment or ghosting from parties | Implement clear milestone tracking; require active engagement checks within the platform | Impact: 5 <br> Likelihood: 4|
Idea Owner disengagement | Clearly define the Idea Owner's ongoing role as a "Product Owner" who must provide continuous feedback, market research, and active participation throughout development | Impact: 4 <br> Likelihood: 4 |
Unfair distribution of rights or exploitation of programmers | Establish clear role agreements before project kickoff that explicitly protect the intellectual and technical contributions of the developers and designers, ensuring shared credit/equity | Impact: 5 <br> Likelihood: 3 |
Lack of experience | Peer learning and continuous knowledge sharing within the team in addition to connecting with experts and use different resources | Impact: 4 <br> Likelihood: 4 |
Legal loopholes in the Terms of Use | Consult with specialized lawyers | Impact: 4 <br> Likelihood: 3 |
Insufficient time for the whole project | Strict task organization, dividing and distributing tasks evenly among team members based on capacity | Impact: 3 <br> Likelihood: 3 |
Marketing | Focusing on social media and public relations | Impact: 3 <br> Likelihood: 4 |
Budgeting constraints | utilize low-cost tools and optimized ad campaigns - ads | Impact: 4 <br> Likelihood: 5 |

**Note:** All identified risks have been evaluated using a standard Risk Assessment Matrix system. Each risk is scored on a scale of 1 to 5 based on two metrics: (Impact / Likelihood), helping us prioritize our mitigation strategies.
